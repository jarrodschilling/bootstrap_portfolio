Projects

Move Your Feet

Download App | Website

Developed for Apple iOS with React Native, TypeScript, and Expo, Move Your Feet (MYF) is a comprehensive daily guide to recovery, helping users stay accountable and focused on their 12 step journey.

The app features push notifications to keep users engaged with their daily commitments and recovery goals. Users can start their day with structured meditation, and readings, and end each evening with reflection and journaling.

MYF simplifies the daily actions essential for working the 12 steps, helping users achieve goals while transforming sponsor guidance into actionable steps.

Video Demo | Github Repo

About Me

What began as a quest to systematically solve issues within my own equity trading process, led me to fall in love with the idea being able to solve other humans' problems with code. My name is Jarrod and I am currently residing just outside of Salt Lake City, UT. Over the past two years I've developed a fondness and curiosity around what I can create with code.

I am an Apple iOS and full-stack software developer with a focus on JavaScript/TypeScript and Python technologies. I specialize in building scalable web applications, primarily using frameworks like Next.js, React, and NestJS on the frontend and backend. My experience spans across designing robust authentication systems, handling SQL and NoSQL databases, and deploying cloud-based solutions on AWS.

With a diverse professional background that includes equity trading and portfolio management, I bring a unique analytical perspective to software development, particularly in fintech and data-driven applications. I have successfully delivered projects like Trade Stats Pro, a trading statistics tracker, and my.Breadth, a stock market breadth analysis tool.

I'm always looking for opportunities to create high-impact software solutions, leveraging my problem-solving skills and passion for clean, maintainable code. I graduated from the University of Florida with a Master of Science in Finance (2008) and a Bachelor of Science in Business Administration (2007). I live life by the four C's: coffee, coding, climbing, and college_football. GO GATORS!
